Footers

You need them!

29/09/2015

Subtitle

Content

Footer

/ˈfʊtə/

a line or block of text appearing at the foot of each page of a book or document.

Footers are one of the most important parts of your site! Isn’t the footer where your users/viewers/customers end up at the end of when they browse? Yes, it is! The William blog outlines well just how much you really need a footer:

Whereas the header and top of the website didn’t compel me to, or help me make any decisions, the footer can.

Therefore, there is a need to make sure that you don’t leave your site’s visitors confused as to where to go at the end of your page; you need to give them somewhere to go. This place to go can come in the form of links across your site, a signup for your newsletter, policies, or whatever things you can dream up to put in.

So far, this has been a short post but I wanted to remind y’all to put some effort and care into your footers because they’re a big part of making your customer’s experience in your site better. Though useful in landing pages, this technique will prove even better and yield more results when used in a site with a lot of pages such as shopping sites. (I must say that I’m very bad at doing this.)
